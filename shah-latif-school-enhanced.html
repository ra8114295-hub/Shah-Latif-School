Our Inspiration

Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai

Sufi Poet & Saint

"Beloved's separation kills me friends,
At His door, many like me, their knees bend."
— Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai

1689Born

Our school is proudly named after Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai (1689–1752) — one of the greatest Sufi scholars and poets in the history of Sindh and all of Pakistan. He was born in Hala Haveli, near Hyderabad, and his shrine stands in Bhit Shah, Matiari.

Shah Latif is best known for his masterpiece Shah Jo Risalo — a collection of poetry that is considered the greatest work of Sindhi literature. His poetry speaks of love for God, respect for humanity, religious tolerance, and the unity of all people.

His lineage traces directly back to the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) through Imam Zain-ul-Abideen. He received early education in Persian and Sindhi, and though largely self-educated, he became deeply versed in Arabic, the Quran, the Hadiths, and the works of Maulana Rumi.

He is referred to by scholars as a "Direct Emanation of Rumi's spirituality in the Indian world." His values of knowledge, tolerance, and love for humanity are the foundation upon which our school is built.

About Our School

Building the Future of Jacobabad

Shah Latif Public Secondary School is one of Jacobabad's respected educational institutions, located on First Family Lane in the heart of the city. We are committed to providing quality education to every student who walks through our doors.

Carrying the name and spirit of Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai, we believe that education is the most powerful gift we can give our children. Like the great Sufi poet himself, we believe in the unity of knowledge, character, and spirituality.

Our school provides a nurturing environment where students from KG through to Matric (Class 10) receive academic instruction, moral guidance, and the confidence they need to succeed in life — in Jacobabad, in Sindh, and beyond.

Core Values Inspired by Shah Latif

01

Knowledge & Wisdom

Shah Latif was largely self-educated yet became one of the greatest scholars. We inspire our students to love learning for life.

02

Religious Tolerance

The major themes of Shah Latif's poetry include religious tolerance and humanistic values — principles we live by every day.

03

Love & Unity

Shah Latif's Risalo speaks of unity of the Almighty and love for all people. We build a community of respect and belonging.

Sindhi Heritage

Community Service
